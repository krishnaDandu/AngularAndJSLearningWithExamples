HISTORY & DESCRIPTION

Litti-Chokha is a special delicacy from Bihar which has gained fame across India. The flavours represent the earthiness of the state which can be found at street stalls, but limited in premium fine-dine.

Famous for its rustic taste, Litti is traditionally made of Atta (whole wheat flour), stuffed with Sattu (bengal gram flour aka desi protein) mixed with home-made spices and baked over coal/wood-fire and finally tossed with Ghee. Chokha is mash of (roasted - brinjal, tomato and potato) and spices along with mustard oil to get that pungent flavour. It is served with green coriander chutney. It is very healthy, as both dishes are completely baked/roasted (except the Ghee part).

People often confuse Litti with closely look alike rajasthani dish - baati. But it is completely different from the latter, in all aspects - taste, texture and preparation.

In various parts, Litti is also served with Chicken and Mutton masala (gravy).
